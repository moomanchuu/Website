::Psychology::

It is well known that Psychology is a "soft" science. What this really means is that the subject being studied is inherently extremely complex, and therefore results are difficult to verify through controlled experiments. In other words, experiments carried out in order to isolate variables are nearly impossible to repeat with consistent outcomes. Such complex structures studied would be human behavior, thought, and societal systems. Theories are then crafted through the use of observation and interpretation.

What has always fascinated me was that we know little about our brain, presumably the incredible organ that differentiates humans from the rest of the animal kingdom. My favorite psychologist is Carl Jung. A Swiss psychologist from the late 19th and early 20th century, his thoughts on the subconscious and human thought are still incredible to read today.

One question that I would like to ask is:
Carl Jung, in his works, extensively discussed the plights of the individual in society and the impact of the subconscious, along with other psychological structures, in ones life. However, Jung died in 1975, before any real use of the internet publicly. Contextualize his works within the modern age. Do his findings and assertions still ring true? The bottom line is asking whether or not Jung discussed the nature of human psychology that is present no matter the era, or discussed the results that the current era he lived had on human thought.
